  • The Delay effect records the audio input and plays it back after a period of time (controlled by the 'Speed' slider).
  • The delayed sound can be repeated a certain number of times. To control the number of delays and their volume, use the 'Length' and 'Level' sliders.
  • The tone of the delayed sound can also be controlled with the 'filter' slider

  • Tremolo gives a 'wavering' effect on the sound. This is achieved by oscillating the volume of the sound back and forth between two values.
  • The depth slider controls the intensity of the effect by altering the minimum volume for each oscillation. The speed slider just controls the time for one oscillation.
  • For the most pronounced version of the effect (the deepest and fastest oscillation), put the 'Depth' and 'Speed' sliders at their lowest levels.
